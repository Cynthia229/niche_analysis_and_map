    From Woodlands Academy of the Sacred Heart

    A Woodlands education is about thinking creatively and critically—asking tough questions, making connections, delving deep into ideas. Your teachers will encourage you to be an active participant in your education. We believe education is a transformative process. With our distinctive combination of academic excellence, opportunity, support, and global studies experience, you will graduate ready to meet whatever challenges and opportunities your future holds with self-confidence and courage.

    I am not going to sugar coat this. I was skeptical of Woodlands at first. It is small, tucked away in Lake Forest, and single gender. Maybe this was an environment that was too protected, sheltered, and "hand holding" for a young woman in high school? And while it is small, the experience has been anything but "too reserved or safe".

    The school is diverse and composed of students from the City, the burbs, and international locations that span the socio-economic spectrum.

    The academics are great and exceed expectation. If a student seeks challenge, the answer is always yes! If a student tries to back away from their potential, a teacher is on it. Students appreciate that WA will not let potential go unfulfilled.

    Girls learn to advocate & take pride in their accomplishments. Upon graduation they leave WA self-sufficient & confident women. They are ready to define their unique place in society.

    Despite school size, college placement is amazing! Deserves all the accolades!

    Rating 5 out of 5  
    Woodlands was the perfect school for my daughter and her development over the four years was tremendous. I have had kids with IEPs at several schools and would say the learning program was one of the keys to her success. Rather than just holding her up, they immediately started empowering her to move beyond the program, which she did. Many such programs teach kids how to survive, this one taught her to thrive. The warm and welcoming environment sparked her to move out of her comfort zone and become a leader both at her school and in the community. Oh, and she had tons of fun too.

    She is off to a top tier University, as are many of her classmates, a testament to the benefits of a customized learning experience and personal attention. I would encourage girls and their parents to check out the school, there's lots to offer, regardless of your background.
