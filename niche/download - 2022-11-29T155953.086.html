    From Holderness School

    Holderness School is more than a school. It’s an elevated experience. We are a dynamic community of learners, adventurers, artists, advocates, and athletes in New Hampshire’s White Mountains. At Holderness, students are respected, known, and challenged. Whether you’re interested in epic outdoor experiences, serving the community in our leadership program, or exploring your intellectual passions, Holderness is committed to the growth and success of each student.

    Snow Sports

    Holderness School has produced 18 Olympic athletes, six World Cup Championship Team members, 15 Collegiate All-Americans, and 38 National Team members. Our snow sports athletes train to compete in the prep school league, and the US Ski & Snowboard, USASA and FIS circuits. There’s a reason why we’re known as the best preparatory school ski program in the nation—we produce winners year after year.

    Rating 3 out of 5  
    There are a lot of things I like about Holderness but there definitely is room for improvement. The adults are very, very nice and they will be the biggest advocate for your success both in school and life after school. It feels like one big happy family by just being here and people are friendly for the most part. The academics are very good although there is a ton of busywork. However, it is very hard for POCs to find their place in the school. People like to assume that we self-segregate but in reality, people do not really make an effort to get to know us because the school is very culturally homogeneous. The dorms on the older side have no common rooms so it may be suffocating at times inside the dorm which was the case for me. I would have loved to come in at a younger age but as an older new kid, I loved some parts but didn’t find it the whole thing the most enjoyable experience. I did made nice friends but I wish people were more open for genuine connection.

    Holderness School is amazing so far. I started off my freshman orientation with a trip hiking, which instantly got me to make connections with my new classmates. The dorm parents/advisors really want the best for you and are so kind and loving. The food is great, the class schedule is great, everything is amazing. There are no cliques at Holderness, everybody talks to everybody on campus. Even though it is an Episcopalian School, Holderness is 100% supportive of any type of religion and doesn't try to force anything on anybody. I would totally recommend checking out Holderness, where you can find home.
