    From The Webb School

    The Webb School has a 150-year history of providing challenging academics with an emphasis on personal integrity in a small family-like setting. The community of 330 students in grades 612 embraces diverse backgrounds, personalities, and interests. Together, they become a part of Webb's great tradition, and with firm loyalty to trust and integrity, grow to become independent, confident, and responsible citizens of the world. The rural and intimate setting promotes a focus on personal values, not materialism, so that student acceptance comes from within.

    Emerging Voices

    Through our Emerging Voices program, students learn to create original ideas, assemble a body of research, and communicate those ideas effectively through writing and public speaking. Students showcase their skills each year by performing various types of presentations. These exhibitions are developed and completed under the mentorship of the students' advisors and other members of the community.

    Rating 3 out of 5  
    We are sadly disillusioned with Webb.
    Positives:
    Gorgeous spread out campus that simulates college life
    Dining hall food is generally good
    College prep is good (getting high school students ready for college studying)
    Some amazing teachers and coaches
    Negatives:
    Disparity in the way students are treated, graded, and disciplined....largely based on whether or not they were recruited to play a sport
    Sub-par college counselling and scholarship application help
    Priority issues concerning facility improvement. Webb just spent a very large amount of money on a turf football field and new track when the dorms are falling apart and there is no bathroom facility at the football field. No football bleachers.
    Concentration on is on recruiting and giving scholarships to outside athletes while the non-star athlete students and non athlete students (whose parents are paying full tuition) are treated differently, not given playing time, not assisted in college recruiting.
    'Clubs' are a joke
