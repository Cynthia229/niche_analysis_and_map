    From Key School

    Key's innovative approach to teaching and learning is what sets us apart from other independent schools. Key is renowned for its rigorous academic program, which emphasizes inquiry-based learning, interdisciplinary studies, and a global perspective, but our students also experience meaningful connections, value and respect the differences of others, and have a positive outlook on learning and their future.

    A diverse, supportive, deliberately small community that values academic excellence and nurtures a love of learning is what makes Key School one of the most sought-after educations in Maryland.

    Rating 2 out of 5  
    In 2020, our son was enrolled in pre-k. We ultimately left due to many differences. Reviews present Key School as an unsurpassed institution of academic learning. Although that may be, prospective parents should be aware of some lesser-known aspects. Key School, its staff, culture and curriculum, are extremely “liberal”. They have a sizable DEI department that seemingly controls much of the institution. Although not likely to be touted on their website, in 2021, Key School unilaterally adopted the “Pollyanna Racial Literacy Curriculum” for 3 to 4-yos without ANY parent involvement. This pedagogy, among other things, focuses on identities, particularly around skin tone. Please do your research and ask plenty of questions. Some aspects of Key may align with your values; others may not.

    My family moved a lot when I was growing up. My four siblings and I all attended Key in the late 80's and early 90's. We ranged from Kindergarten to Upper School. While we moved out of state after three years, all of us still consider Key our favorite school of all--and we moved a lot! Now I am a parent of two boys at Key and get to be reminded every day why Key was so special to my family decades ago. The core of what makes Key special hasn't changed, but they have evolved programming and take an innovative approach to teaching and learning. My children will not be rote-memorization machines, they will be critical thinkers, creative problem solvers and really resourceful and respectful members of society.
