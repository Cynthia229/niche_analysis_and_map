    From Sidwell Friends School

    Sidwell Friends School is a PK–12, co-educational Quaker day school founded in 1883. Individually and collectively, we challenge ourselves to pursue excellence in academic, athletic, and artistic realms. We are committed to the joys of exploration and discovery. Differences among us enhance intellectual inquiry, expand understanding, and deepen empathy. The Quaker pillars of the School inspire active engagement in environmental stewardship, global citizenship, and service. We find strength in reflection and shared silence. At the heart of each endeavor, we strive to discern deeper truths about ourselves and our common humanity, preparing students and adults to “let their lives speak.”

    Rating 5 out of 5  
    I loved my time at Sidwell. When my college counselor asked me what I would like to do once I graduated if I could do anything, I said I would spend another four years at Sidwell. Sidwell fostered a tight-knit community the like of which I doubt I will find again elsewhere. I started at Sidwell in 7th grade and by the time I graduated, I confidently considered every one of my peers a close friend.

    Sidwell's main flaws stem from the pressure to be *that* school. For example, student and family pressure to maintain the highest quality STEM courses and college prep often leads the administration to overlook arts and humanities (especially arts). This isn't to say the arts and humanities classes I took at Sidwell were poor, in fact, they were exceptional. But sometimes it is hard to forget the school's high reputation.

    If there is one way I can express how much I loved my time there it is this. My career ambition is to one day teach at Sidwell, the school I loved so much.
