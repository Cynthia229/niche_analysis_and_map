    From The Post Oak School

    Post Oak offers a comprehensive education from infancy through high school, which includes the International Baccalaureate diploma program for grades 11 and 12. Students at each level benefit from carefully prepared environments, including a second campus dedicated to adolescent development that accesses partner institutions in the Museum District, Texas Medical Center, and downtown. As lifelong learners, our students develop academic, social, and entrepreneurial skills that help them pursue their passions and live purposeful, successful lives.

    More About The Post Oak School Academics

    Mixed-Year Age Groups

    In Post Oak's learning environments, mixed-age groups (with two- to three-year-age ranges) allow students to take on a series of roles in the classroom, from mentee to mentor. Under the guidance of their teacher, students come to school to live together, learn from each other, and engage in this collaborative process.

    I transferred to POHS as a junior, and I loved my experience (even with COVID.) The hands off approach attracts intelligent yet laid back students and the environment is anything but toxic. Teachers care a lot about you and are open to helping you inside and outside the classroom. The reason I give four not five stars is because I wish the academics were a bit more rigorous - I got my IB diploma but I heard most of the class did not/did not care about getting the diploma. I suppose this is the trade off for a laid back environment.

    The school itself is great, I love the community style space and I felt fully prepared for college in every aspect.
