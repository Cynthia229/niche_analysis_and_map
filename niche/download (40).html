    From Illinois Mathematics and Science Academy

    Established as a public STEM academy for Illinois students, the Illinois Mathematics and Science Academy (IMSA) is internationally recognized for inspiring, challenging, and nurturing students who use their exceptional abilities to improve people’s lives. IMSA’s academically advanced residential program enables exceptional students in grades 10-12 to reach extraordinary levels of achievement.

    Our mission is to ignite and nurture creative, ethical, scientific minds that advance the human condition. We are a diverse community that promotes curiosity and exploration. We’re about more than great college prospects and great jobs, we’re about making our families proud. We’re about a better world for everyone through immersive learning experiences.

    At IMSA’s residential Academy, you will be part of a community of around 650 diverse students who are passionate about learning. When you live alongside others, you create life-long friendships. You build your confidence and self-reliance.

    Student Inquiry and Research (SIR)

    Student Inquiry and Research (SIR) supports student investigations conducted on and off-campus. The SIR department provides a framework for students to conduct original investigations on compelling questions of interest, collaborate with other students and professional researchers, and to share their investigation results through public presentations and publications.

    Rating 3 out of 5  
    The best aspect of this school by far was the other students - I made so many life-long friends that I never would have been able to at a regular school. There were also a ton of amazing student-run events that made going to this school worth it. The extracurricular (non-athletic) activities were also pretty good, and you were able to get a diversity of clubs that you would normally only get at a large school while still having the small-school experience (250 students/grade across 3 grades), and the range of classes was also pretty good for the size of the school, with more interesting topics than most smaller schools have. However, the academics were extremely rigorous, and support from the school administration was definitely lacking, leading to a highly stressful high school experience. To succeed at IMSA, you generally have to be a combination of extroverted, independent, and intelligent, and if you're not a certified prodigy genius, expect to heavily lean on your peers for help.

They do offer a challenging education for their students and provide an atmosphere similar to college. The administration, however, has been going downhill. Racial discriminations has been an issue at school but admin doesn't solve anything when they claim they do. The academics are good but in order to perform well at IMSA, you have to be independent in your studies. The teachers at IMSA are mostly there to answer questions rather than teach the entire class, specifically in the Math Department. For this reason, its important to pay attention in class or you'll fall behind very easily. Independence is also important because IMSA is a residential school.
