    From Saddle River Day School

    At Saddle River Day School we are passionately committed to preparing our students not only for college, but for life beyond formal education. We maintain small class sizes and excellent student-teacher ratios so that we can help each child realize his or her potential with confidence. We challenge each student as an individual, knowing that every person acquires skills and maturity at a different pace. We help our students engage in intellectual risk-taking at all ages by caring deeply for them as the individuals they are and by nurturing their souls as well as challenging their minds. We empower students with the tools, space, and encouragement to find their passions and thrive. We teach our students how to think and not what to think. At SRDS, we differentiate ourselves by balancing the familiarity of a small school with the programmatic slate of a big one. The result? Graduates who own their future.

    I was a student at the SRDS school. It was an ok school until about 2013 or 2012. The school became a place of judgment and sorting. If you were a quiet kid, you were left out of almost everything. It became a place where if you didn't fit the mold, you don't belong. Now don't get me started on some of the teachers. Most of them were really kind and they cared about the students; then there is that 5% of teachers that are rude, un-kind, and favored students. The programs such as art, science, and music; it was amazing and fun! Then math and social studies came into play. The teachers were giving out 3 hours of homework a night, and it was too advanced. There was 11th grade math given to a fifth grader. Really? How is that ok? Social studies was all over the place and no one learned anything. The lessons were incomplete and sometimes too difficult to understand. Be careful when sending a kid to the school. If your kid is social and smart, then ok, but if not; please don't come here.

    Rating 4 out of 5  
    Saddle River Day School is different than most schools considering there is only 47 kids in each grade. I love that most if not all of my classes are very small and I get to connect with my teachers really well and as a shy, timid person, it has helped me branch out and make me feel more comfortable with participating during class and asking questions. I have learned to become an independent individual and love that there is an honesty policy. My teachers and counselors really care for me and my future and help me make connections. SRDS is a small community that prepares you for college and teaches you how to become more independent. Although there are so many great things about my school, one thing I would change would be the way other sports teams are treated in my school besides basketball. Basketball is the main sport in my school however, all the other teams are disregarded even though they are equally successful. As a member of the lacrosse and soccer team, it is frustrating.
