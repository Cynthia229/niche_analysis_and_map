    Into the Wild - Poly's Outdoor Education Program

    Through exploration, adventure, and physical challenge, Poly's Outdoor Ed program for Middle School and Upper School students develop their self-confidence, sense of personal responsibility, resilience, and appreciation for the natural world. Students participate in trips that explore a variety of environments including alpine, desert, and water-based expeditions.

    Rating 3 out of 5  
    I have mixed feelings about Poly. I'm currently a senior and I've been here since kindergarten. I've gotten a lot out of the school academically and I think I'm very well prepared for college now, but socially Poly can be a little backward. You make friends in kindergarten and stick with that pretty much until you graduate. While you develop strong connections with your classmates, you never really learn how to make friends. To any parent considering enrolling your kid here, please wait until middle or high school when they can decide if the courseload and weird social atmosphere is for them. I've heard coming in later can be tough with everyone already having such solid friend groups though, so look out for that as well.

    Rating 5 out of 5  
    As a parent of current poly students, I can attest to the quality of the teachers, faculty, student body and facilities. Poly is academically rigorous and sets high expectations for its students while providing the scholastic and emotional support needed to ensure every student has an opportunity to succeed. Poly also does an admirable job incorporating character building into their curriculum. Compassion, service, self awareness, diversity,
    inclusion, tolerance and integrity are all modeled and reinforced throughout the campus at every grade level. Poly can of course continue to work on its diversity by reaching out to the larger Los Angles community to attract a more broad coalition of students. That said, we could not be happier. Most importantly our children (middle and high school) feel happy, proud, challenged, accepted, supported and seen.
