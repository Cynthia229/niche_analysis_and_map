    From The Waterford School

    Student learning is Waterford Schools reason for being. Since its founding, the School has instilled habits of independent thought, critical thinking, responsibility, passionate learning, and service to others. Waterford Schools program provides a strong foundation distinguished by a rich curriculum, an outstanding faculty, and a deep commitment to student growth. The learning environment is caring, nurturing, and inspiring, and the campus culture prepares students to pursue lives of meaning and purpose. Waterford delivers a world-class liberal arts education that stimulates intellect, ignites passion, and shapes character.

      Lower School Open House

      January 18, 6:00 pm

      Waterford School 1480 E 9400 S, Sandy, UT 84093

      View Event Description

      We invite families with children in preschool (ages 3-4) through Class V to join us for a Lower School Open House. The Open House will include a brief introduction to the school, then guests will be invited to tour the classrooms, including homerooms, science, art, dance, music, computers and P.E. Teachers from each grade level and specialty will be available to answer questions. Waterford administration and current parents will also be present. Children are welcome to attend with parents. If you have middle or high school students, please call the Admissions Office at 801-816-2216 to schedule a personalized visit and tour.

    Our daughter started at Waterford in the PK3 class, the first the school offers. We wanted to find a place in Salt Lake City that offered a great education, diverse student body, and caring faculty and staff. Waterford offered us all that and more.

    From the moment you walk into the building, it's clear Waterford is just...different. The students all greet you with a smile and a polite "hello" as they walk down the halls. The administration knows every parent and greets them by name whenever you encounter them. The teachers truly care about the students, their achievement, and their well-being.

    If you are in the Salt Lake area and are looking for something different, take a look at Waterford. We highly recommend it.

    Rating 3 out of 5  
    Waterford cares about their image to the outside world far more than they care about the actual students in the school. Not to say it isn't a good school. I have gone here since 3rd grade and I have discovered that par for par I think students from Waterford tend to be more mature and ready for the world of advanced studies than their other schools counterparts. The school does have many problems, however. Even when students show focuses outside of the arts the school still puts all funding in the art department and gives grades depending on your finished project looks rather than your effort and attempt. Waterford's teachers really want the best for their students but the administration isn't the same. They are the ones protecting the image of the school. The final comment is the school refuses to punish anyone harshly regardless of crime. They see a student as 25k extra cash and an expulsion just a removal of that cash when they could just ignore the problem.
