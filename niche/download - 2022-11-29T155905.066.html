    From The Galloway School

    Located in Atlanta, Georgia, The Galloway School is an independent day school that serves a diverse student body of 750 students, age 3 through grade 12. The Galloway community is one where learning is joyful, individuals are valued, and self-discovery is encouraged. Galloway students confidently embrace challenges while developing the knowledge, skills, and cultural competence to thrive as enlightened contributors in their chosen pathways.

    Rating 2 out of 5  
    One child attended Galloway for years (my other children attended other local schools). My kids have no behavior problems or learning disabilities and are academically high-achieving. Galloway has some warm and wonderful teachers who care (same as other kids' schools). Galloway also has a far higher number of incompetent teachers, caused by poor hiring practices and a failure to hold teachers accountable. This problem has persisted despite numerous changes in leadership. Parents and kids are friendlier and less cliquey than at other schools. Students who excel in sports and the arts are celebrated, however, while those who focus on academic achievement feel marginalized by administration. Galloway's culture, leadership, and college counselors promote underachievement. If you express valid concerns about the curriculum, you are met with defensiveness/hostility and are "encouraged" to leave. Galloway spends too much time and energy cultivating its image; not enough on substance.
