    From Gould Academy

    Ranked #1 Boarding School in Maine by Niche in 2021 and 2022

    Gould is a small, co-ed, independent boarding school that is passionate about developing academically motivated students that are prepared for college and helping them to become independent-minded, ethical citizens who will lead lives of purpose, action, excellence, and compassion in a dynamic world.

    Our unique location nestled in the mountains of western Maine allows our students opportunities to learn beyond the classroom and enjoy a personalized, hands-on educational experience exploring the world that surrounds us. Within our inclusive community, that balances tradition with innovation, our students are encouraged to think freely and challenge themselves. We believe that where there is risk, there is reward, and our dedicated faculty and vast network of alumni provide inspiration and support to help each student thrive.

    One student at a time, we help each become the best version of themselves.

    Upcoming Events

    1. Dec9

      Open House

      December 9, 9:00 am

      Bethel, Maine

      View Event Description

      Open House Events offer students and their families a great opportunity to visit campus, meet faculty and students, and experience our community. While you're here you'll meet and see the many people, programs, and places that make up Gould. The day begins at 9:30 am with a welcome from our Admissions team and Head of School. Then a student tour guide will join each family for a personal tour of our campus, including the IDEAS Center, Art Cottage, Field House, High Performance Center, and Library. Next is lunch in our dining hall with a faculty member, followed by a group discussion with a faculty panel. The structured portion of the day ends at 2 pm. Interviews can then be scheduled by appointment if desired. Be sure to check out the school store to get your Gould gear!

    Winter Term Program

    Goulds Winter Term program is designed for competitive Alpine, Freestyle/Freeride, and Snowboarding student-athletes who want to maximize their winter on snow and in the classroom. This program is exclusive for seventh- and eighth-grade student-athletes and runs from mid-November through the end of competitions in March.

    As a day student, Gould offered me a much more diverse learning environment and curriculum. I found that having a wide range of students from around the world, made learning that much more meaningful, given that some of them had or were experiencing some of the events that we discussed. It made it more tangible and "real", something most schools don't (because they can't) offer. The faculty wasn't quite as diverse, but my German teacher was a resident of Holland, and recounted the sound marching boots made on the cobblestones as German soldiers invaded her dutch hometown.
    Teammates on my baseball team were from Japan, China, Bermuda, Venezuela, Bermuda and Kuwait to name a few. And of course, Bethel, Maine!
    A wonderful, life impacting experience in a beautiful environment, that encapsulated some of richest learnings and best years of my academic life.
