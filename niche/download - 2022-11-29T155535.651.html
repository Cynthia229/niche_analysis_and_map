    Rating 4 out of 5  
    I am a senior at Academic Magnet High School and have attended all four years. This school is academically rigorous and pushes its students to succeed. The school's community is unified and safe in nature and the teachers and staff always have the student population's best interest in mind. Sports and activities are highly active between students, staff, and parents, and they foster a prosperous environment for the school. Students are considerate and kind to one another for the most part. One thing this school must work on is prioritizing racial, cultural, socioeconomic diversity. Academic Magnet is attended by predominantly white, wealthy students while being located in a poorer and historically black area. Seeing the lack of people of color in the student body is discouraging to the school district's educational system. Ultimately, the school is strong in its community and academic value and is well deserving of its #2 high school in the country title.

    Rating 4 out of 5  
    The Academic Magnet High School is definitely something to consider applying to, or consider attending if you have already been accepted. It is generally a great experience, especially with the courses and overall academics. It is noticably smaller than other larger public schools, but the school suits most people who are dedacated in their studies or who have already been accepted. It is competative though, and there will be times where you'll compare yourself to others. For me, it was mostly during my freshman year. Besides that, there is an adequate variety of sport teams. The teams may get competative for you as well, and I would advise to participate in sports you have at least some sort of familiarity with already. If not, I would definitely research some of the basics of the certain sport you are interested in trying. Ultimately, this school can get competative, but I like to think that it's worth it in the end. Besides some social troubles, the academics are nearly flawless!

    Rating 4 out of 5  
    The school is something that has the ability to be great. There is a devoted and intelligent student body who prioritize learning but can also enjoy the high school experience. While the majority of sports teams aren't great, the student involvement is something a person would expect of a school twice the size. There're are a variety of clubs and fun spirit weeks that make the school more enjoyable. The academics are relatively difficult but the majority of AP classes prepare you well for the test. It is evident that most teachers care about their students and want them to succeed. The school has some problems with the administration and it often leaves students frustrated. While the school has its many strengths it has other faults and often doesn't entirely live up to the positive reputation it's been given.
