    From The Bishop's School

    Bishops is an Episcopal school that nurtures students of intellect and character to think independently, learn collaboratively, grow their sense of self and act with greater purpose. Our vision is the pursuit of the question: How do our students learn and grow best? With core values of integrity, compassion, inclusion, intellect, excellence and justice, our community includes students from 90+ schools representing diverse religious, cultural and socioeconomic backgrounds. 20 percent of our student body receives more than $4.1 million in financial aid; campus life offers 72 sports teams, 40 performing arts shows in dance, theater, choral and instrumental music, visual arts options from photography to animation and more. Classrooms are designed for interaction and conversation rather than lecture. Bishops is an inclusive community that believes our differences are a source of strength.

    Creative Sciences - Cross-Curricular Collaboration

    Bishops Center for the Creative Sciences serves as a think-tank for STEAM and interdisciplinary education. Large and small-scale 3D printers, a laser cutter and a CNC router are available in our maker space to help students create, make and innovate. Every department on campus uses the Center to design and create materials for classwork, classrooms, props for theater productions and more!

    I loved my experience at Bishops. In terms of academics, I know that I was challenged every single day and taught to think critically. I feel more than prepared for my new college work. Additionally, the Bishops community ad a whole is incredibly kind and welcoming. There are only a couple things you should know before enrolling here. 1) Because the academics are very challenging, there is more grade deflation compared to other high schools. It is really hard to stand out academically. 2) If you are looking for a traditional high school experience, we lack in spirit and sports. Overall, the people, academics, college prep/counseling, and small classes make Bishops a school that I would highly recommend.

    Rating 4 out of 5  
    Bishop's is a good place for self-motivated people to take advantage of the variety of resources. With the Center for Creative Sciences, access to the Adobe suite, and other opportunities, it is easy to discover activities that aren't usually available to high schoolers. There is a campus attitude of pressure to do well, which can be benign if families and students are on the same page of being proud of themselves for their effort vs. always trying to "catch up" with everyone else. For anyone considering going to Bishop's, my advice is to not psych yourself out of a class because a teacher won't give you an A; first of all, that usually isn't true - you just have to put in more effort. Secondly, my favorite classes were the higher level ones in the English and STEM departments (except comp. science). In short, Bishop's is a great place for people who aren't afraid to try new things and take advantage of available resources!
