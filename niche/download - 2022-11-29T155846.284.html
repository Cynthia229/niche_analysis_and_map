    Rating 4 out of 5  
    Coppell High School is a highly diverse and competitive learning environment that encourages students to challenge themselves academically, socially, and in their community. I feel the opportunities given to me in terms of access to top-level programs, high-caliber classes, and a welcoming nature is one that should not be taken for granted and encourages me to take my experiences far. I have built strong relationships with students and teachers at Coppell and taught a significant amount about understanding others and experiences unlike my own. However, the competitive nature of the students can create a stressful and superficial environment, in which some are only looking to achieve things for themselves, and will go to many lengths to create an exceptional life on paper. While I have enjoyed my experience thus far, there are certainly aspects of the school in terms of monitoring teacher quality and fairness and creating a positive environment for students that could improve.

    Rating 3 out of 5  
    I loved my high school experience for the most part. Coppell is a highly diverse high school so find your clique here is very easy. However people in some cliques often got opportunities that others would not. The school culture is far from "friendly". I know the administration tries its best to keep up with students but there are so many people resulting in a poor administrative structure. The students of this school are increasing by the hundreds every year. They need to find a way to accommodate more and more people. There are a few teachers that deserve nothing but praise like my AP bio teacher, but then there are others who should not be qualified to teach. Academics come before all else, and there are students and parents who make it their whole life. Optimistically I can say that it prepared me for real life but I still don't think its worth sacrificing your high school experience.
